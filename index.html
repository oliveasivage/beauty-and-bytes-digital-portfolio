💄 Sephora Foundation Equity Analysis

💡 Research Question

Are Sephora foundation products equally serving customers across different skin tones?

🌍 Project Overview

This project analyzes 379 customer reviews scraped from Sephora’s top foundation products. The goal is to evaluate inclusivity and performance across skin tone and skin type using statistical methods. The dataset was created by scraping the top 5 most recent reviews on Sephora's bestselling foundations.

📊 Overall Ratings

Mean rating: 3.56 | Median: 4 | Mode: 5 Overall sentiment is positive, but some negative experiences exist.

🎨 Skin Tone Analysis

ANOVA shows no statistically significant difference across skin tones (p = 0.3678). Sephora performs consistently across tones.

🧴 Skin Type Analysis

Significant differences exist across skin types (p = 0.0095). Product performance varies depending on skin condition.

💬 Sentiment Analysis

Most reviews are positive, but negative sentiment is concentrated in specific groups.

🧠 Text Insights

Customers frequently mention undertones, coverage, and skin compatibility.

🔍 Topic Modeling

Three key themes emerged: product performance, skin compatibility, and shade matching.

⏱️ Review Recency

Reviews are recent (median = 6 days), making findings highly relevant.

💬 What Customers Are Saying

Beyond ratings, customer reviews reveal important patterns in how products are actually experienced. Across all skin tones, customers frequently mention shade matching, undertones, and coverage, which suggests that Sephora’s shade range is a key strength.

However, many negative reviews focus on performance issues such as dryness, separation, and texture. These concerns appear more often among certain skin types, especially dry and sensitive skin, which aligns with the statistical findings showing significant differences across skin types.

Positive reviews often highlight lightweight feel, blendability, and natural finish, while negative reviews tend to describe issues with wear time, oxidation, or how the product interacts with skin texture.

This suggests that while customers are generally able to find suitable shades, the formula itself may not perform equally well for all skin conditions.

📌 Final Conclusion

This analysis finds no statistically significant differences in customer satisfaction across skin tones, suggesting that Sephora foundation products are performing consistently in terms of shade inclusivity.

However, both statistical testing and review text analysis indicate that customer experience varies significantly across skin types. While many users report positive experiences, others frequently describe issues related to texture, wear, and product performance.

These findings suggest that Sephora has been successful in addressing shade diversity, but there is an opportunity to improve formulation performance across different skin conditions.

Overall, this project demonstrates how combining quantitative analysis with customer review data can provide a more complete understanding of product performance and highlight opportunities for improvement.
